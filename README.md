Uber Ride Operations — End-to-End Analytics Project

Overview

End-to-end data analytics project analyzing Uber ride operations across 4 major U.S. cities (Chicago, Houston, Los Angeles, New York) from January 2022 to June 2024.

Tools Used

MySQL — Data analysis and querying - Full MySQL script with 25+ queries
Power BI — Dashboard and data visualization - Dashboard screenshot

 What Was Analyzed
Overview Metrics

Total trips, revenue, and average fare
Average driver and rider ratings
Revenue and trips per city

Operational Insights

Average trip duration and distance by city
Cancellation reasons and percentages
Peak hours for ride demand
Payment method success rates

Strategic Insights

Top 5 drivers by revenue
Surge pricing impact on average fare
Zones with highest pickup demand
Drivers above average rating per city

Trend Analysis

Month-over-month trip and revenue growth
Moving average to detect high-demand days
Drivers with declining activity over time

Segmentation

Riders segmented by frequency (High / Medium / Low)
Top 10% riders by total spending
Driver performance tiers (Top / Good / Low)

Key Findings

Houston generated the highest total revenue
Personal emergencies were the #1 cancellation reason
Surge pricing trips averaged ~$60 vs ~$30 without surge
Card was the dominant payment method by revenue
